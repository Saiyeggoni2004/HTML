When styled with a background colour, this block-level behaviour becomes evident. For example, if you had two `div` elements, even with minimal content, they would stack vertically: ```html

``` In this context, the mention of `div` being "elementary separated" highlights its characteristic of creating a distinct, separated block of content. Applying a "background green" or any other background colour to a `div` would show this block covering the entire width of its container. * **`span` (Inline Element):** A `span` element does not start on a new line and only takes up as much width as its content requires. It's used to apply styles or manipulate a small portion of content within a larger line of text. For example, if you wanted to highlight a specific word or phrase within a paragraph: ```html

``` Here, the "background green" (or `lightgreen` in this case) would only appear behind the words "highlighted text", demonstrating `span`'s inline nature without disrupting the flow of the paragraph.
